History

The idea for the ScroOoge is borrowed from Martin Kleppmann who wrote the first "Scrooge" - a command line based perl program. His program became popular amongst his friends at Corpus Christi College in Cambridge (England) in 2005 [please correct me if this is wrong!]. At its hights about 30 people have been using it [please correct me again!]. Unfortunately the number of active users decreased to only a handful as the friends graduated from university.

The Current ScroOoge

The current web ScroOoge was started by Patrick Dietrich when working with Martin in his company (Ept Computing) in Cambridge in 2008. The design is written by Jens Scherbl who met Patrick during their time in Ulm (Germany) (have a look at some of Jens' other Projects here: http://www.instantbonsai.net)

We hope that you enjoy using the ScroOoge and that it helps you solve a common problem. Please let us know, what you like or don't like about the ScroOoge to help us making it as useful as possible!

Patrick and Jens
